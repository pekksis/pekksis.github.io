Rules of the Road

A summary for the bold and the reckless
⁂

The Skill Roll

When you attempt something with a real chance of failure, the GM calls for a Skill Roll. Build your dice pool, roll everything at once, and count successes.

Step 1 — Build Your Pool

  • Attribute Die — reflects raw capability, set by the skill's linked attribute. Ranges from d12 (weakest) to 2d8 (exceptional).
  • Fate Dice — one per success the GM requires. Default: d10 each.

Step 2 — Count Successes

This is a roll-under system. Smaller numbers win, and smaller dice are better. Any die at or below your Skill Level counts as one success. Meet the required number and you succeed.

Your character must leap a crumbling stone bridge. Jumping uses Agility (d8). The GM requires 2 successes, so you add 2 Fate dice. An ally on the far side is ready to catch you — the GM grants Advantage, shrinking the Fate dice to d8.

You roll your d8 and two d8s, getting 3, 5, and 7. Jumping Skill Level is 4. Only the 3 qualifies. 1 success. You needed 2. You fail by one.

Advantage & Disadvantage

Favourable conditions may earn Advantage, shrinking Fate dice to d8. Unfavourable conditions bring Disadvantage, inflating them to d12.

↳ Failing by One

Fall exactly one success short and you are not finished. Choose one:

Push the Roll Reroll one failed die and describe the desperate effort your character makes. Fail again after Pushing and the consequences are far worse than a clean failure.
Succeed at a Cost You get what you wanted, but the GM names a price. A lost weapon, a raised alarm, an opened wound. You decide if it is worth paying.
Accept Failure Take the standard consequences and move on.
You fail by one on the bridge jump. You ask for a Consequence. The GM rules: you make the jump, but land hard and drop your weapon into the chasm. Across — but unarmed.
Instead you Push: bracing your boots against the last stone, you hurl yourself forward. You reroll the d8 that showed 5 — it comes up 2. Two successes. Made it.

Helping & Group Rolls

For most tasks one player rolls on behalf of the group. Others can lend their Attribute die to the roll if they can justify the help. Some situations require everyone to roll individually — leaping a chasm, swimming a vortex. Failure is then personal.

Skill Advancement

Roll a natural 1 on any skill check to earn Skill XP. Enough XP and the skill advances one level.

Combat

Combat uses a similar roll-under mechanic but with a fixed Target Number. The Target number is usually 4 for both players and enemies. In combat, you roll your two weapon Attribute dice plus two Fate dice and count results at or below the Target as successes.

Actions

On your turn you can perform one Slow action and one Fast action, or two Fast actions. Helping another character perform an action costs you one action of the same type — and you must declare it before any dice are rolled.

Slow Actions

  • Attack with a weapon
  • Unarmed attack
  • Grapple
  • Persuade
  • Taunt
  • Cast a spell
  • Flee

Fast Actions

  • Draw weapon
  • Get up
  • Feint
  • Move
  • Disengage
  • Reload
  • Aim
  • Use item

Outside your turn you may also Parry or Avoid incoming attacks — see below.

Parry vs Avoid

When attacked, declare your response before any dice are rolled.

Parry

Weapon or shield meets the blow. Both sides roll. Your successes cancel the attacker's — and if you come out ahead, you deal damage and apply combat effects right back. Requires a weapon capable of parrying or a shield. Once per round.

Avoid

You get clear of the attack. Your successes cancel the attacker's, but that is all — no counter-damage, no effects. Requires room to move. Unlimited, but each Avoid after the first may carry a penalty.

Attack Outcomes

Subtract defender successes from attacker successes. The net result:

Net 0Locked blades. Both roll Primary Attribute; the lower die wins +1.
Def +1Defender deals damage back. (Parry only)
Att +1Partial hit. Roll weapon damage once.
Def +2Attacker overextends. Defender rolls damage once and applies a combat effect. (Parry only)
Att +2Solid hit. Roll damage twice and pick a combat effect.
Att +3Decisive hit. Roll damage twice and choose a Major combat effect.
You attack with a shortsword (Strength d8, Finesse d10). You roll 2, 7, 4, 9. Target Number 4 — the 2 and 4 count. 2 attacker successes.

Your opponent Avoids and rolls 1 success. Net: Attacker +1. Partial hit. Roll 1d6 damage.
Same attack, but your opponent Parries and rolls 2 successes. Net: 0. Locked blades. Both roll Primary Attribute.

Passive Saves

Some effects trigger an automatic saving roll in Fortitude, Evasion or Willpower. Roll the relevant attribute plus Fate dice against your Save score. Each success reduces the effect by one.

An enemy casts Lightning Bolt with 3 successes, dealing 1d8 per success. You roll Agility and 2 Fate dice against your Evasion score and get 2 successes. The bolt deals 1d8 instead of 3d8.

Wounds & Injuries

Every character can absorb Wounds equal to their Toughness. Once their Wounds exceed their Toughness, they suffer an Injury and Wounds reset to 0. If a single hit deals damage exceeding your Toughness, you suffer 2 Injuries instead.

Taking Damage

Wounds ≤ Toughness Mark the wounds.
Wounds exceed Toughness 1 Injury. Wounds reset to 0.
Damage > Toughness 2 Injuries. Wounds reset to 0.
Damage > 2× Toughness 2 Injuries and immediately at Death's Door.

Injuries

Each Injury sends you to the Injury Table. Effects such as a lost reaction or an Attribute die inflated to d20 last until the adventure ends. They cannot be healed mid-adventure except by rare magic. Wounds can be treated between fights.
